The Pay Gap Across the Atlantic

Same jobs, different economies. Americans earn 2.1x more on average than Brits for equivalent roles. But the gap isn't uniform — some jobs are near parity, others are 5x apart.

Key Finding
The US-UK pay gap widens as you earn more. At the 10th percentile, Americans earn 1.7x more. At the 90th percentile, it's 2.4x. High earners in the US pull dramatically further ahead than their UK counterparts.

The Floor Gap
The lowest-paying US jobs ($31K) earn more than the UK median (£34K). UK bar staff earn just £9,166 — partly reflecting higher part-time work rates in the UK hospitality sector.

Structural Difference
The US economy is 4.3x larger by workforce (143M vs 33M). But the composition differs: the US has far more hand laborers (7M) and food service workers (5M) as distinct categories, while the UK's largest single occupation is care workers (911K) — reflecting the NHS and social care system.
