The Pay Gap Across the Atlantic

Same jobs, different economies. 143M American workers vs 33M British. Every dollar, every job, every percentile — compared. The numbers tell a story about two very different labor markets.

The Headline
Americans earn 2.1x more than Brits for equivalent roles on average. Even after adjusting for purchasing power parity (PPP), the gap is still 1.45x. The gap widens dramatically at the top — from 1.7x at the 10th percentile to 2.4x at the 90th.
But it's not uniform
Marketing directors: 5.2x ($159K vs £30K). Construction painters: 1.1x ($48K vs £44K). Some jobs are near parity. The pattern: physical trades converge, knowledge work diverges massively. The more digital the job, the bigger the US premium.
The Floor Gap
The lowest-paying US occupations ($31K) earn more than the UK national median (£34K). UK bar staff earn £9,166 and cleaners earn £11,852 — partly reflecting the UK's higher rate of part-time work, but the raw numbers are stark.

What this shows
US pay is spread across a wide range ($20K–$240K), while UK pay is tightly compressed into the £20K–£50K band. The US has a much longer right tail — more high-paying outliers. This isn't just exchange rates; it reflects fundamentally different pay structures.

Note on UK low pay
UK ASHE data reports median pay including part-time workers. Roles like bar staff (£9K) and exam invigilators (£1.9K) appear extremely low because many workers are part-time. US BLS reports full-time equivalent pay, making direct comparison at the bottom end misleading. The gap is real but smaller than the raw numbers suggest.

Structural difference
The US workforce is 4.3x larger (143M vs 33M). The US categorizes "hand laborers" as a 7M-worker mega-category; the UK's largest single occupation is care workers (911K) — reflecting the NHS and social care system. The US has far more occupational granularity in physical labor.

The degree premium
A doctoral degree earns 2.9x what "no formal credential" pays ($135K vs $47K). But the biggest jump is from high school to bachelor's — a 71% increase. Master's degrees barely out-earn bachelor's ($90K vs $93K), suggesting diminishing returns outside medicine and law.

The pattern
The fastest-growing jobs are physical/green energy (wind +50%, solar +42%) and healthcare (nurse practitioners +35%). The fastest-declining are overwhelmingly digital admin — tellers, desktop publishers, bill collectors. AI and automation are reshaping both tails.
